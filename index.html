About the Museum

Step into a sanctuary of peace and wonder at the Mahatma Gandhi Museum. Tucked away within the historic Canara High School campus, this "hidden gem" of Mangaluru offers a unique journey through the life of the Father of the Nation. Beyond its moving tribute to Gandhian philosophy, the museum is a fascinating "cabinet of curiosities," housing everything from ancient stone-age tools and rare international coins to exotic natural history specimens.

History & Heritage

The Mahatma Gandhi Museum stands as a living testament to India’s freedom struggle and Gandhian values. Its roots trace back to Mahatma Gandhi’s visits to the Canara institutions, which left a lasting imprint on the cultural and educational life of Mangaluru.

Gandhi first visited the Canara High School in 1927, where he unveiled the portrait of Lokmanya Tilak, inspiring students and citizens alike. His second visit in 1934 was historic, as he laid the foundation stone for the Krishna Mandir within the campus, symbolizing the harmony between education, spirituality, and national service.

In 1939, Gandhi personally granted permission for the museum to be named in his honour—an exceptional gesture reflecting his trust in the institution’s commitment to ethical values. Since then, the museum has carefully preserved rare memorabilia, including artifacts directly associated with Gandhi and his visits.

The museum also highlights the Mushti Fund, an innovative community-supported initiative introduced by Subbarao Pai. By encouraging households to contribute a handful of rice each day to support education, the scheme embodied Gandhian ideals of self-reliance, cooperation, and social responsibility.

Historic photographs and artifacts—such as the laying of the foundation stone of the Canara High School building in 1960—illustrate how Gandhian principles continued to guide institutional growth. Together, these exhibits connect the region’s past with its present, preserving Mangaluru’s rich heritage for future generations.

Educational & Social Value

The Mahatma Gandhi Museum plays a vital role in value-based education. It serves as a learning resource for students, researchers, and the public, encouraging ethical thinking and responsible citizenship.

By highlighting initiatives such as the Mushti Fund and community-led educational movements, the museum demonstrates the practical application of Gandhian principles in social development.
